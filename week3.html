Most of this week's work was centered around data sets and data manipulation, be it with our movie recommendation problem or most of the viewing material, from Machine Learning (Hype!) to quantum computing, which honestly always gets me excited to think about, I really hope one day I can work with one they seem super fun.

The pale blue dot, moonshot thinking & more!

Short videos, true, but their messages to me seem so important, especially these days, we only have one place in this vast universe that we can actually call home and the way we’ve been living is destroying our only actual safe haven.

Now moonshot thinking is really interesting, of course it seems impossible at first, hell, it even seemed counterintuitive to me at first! But the way Astro Teller explained the idea made me think. That HUGE fact he stated early in the video seemed really crazy. How can it be easier to make something 10x better than 10% better? It’s all about the learning moment, yes it’s almost like failure but learning in itself is not true failure, true failure is not even trying or as Astro explained failure is when you continue on a project or idea that you KNEW 6 months earlier could have been dropped to pursue a new and, possibly, better idea. The act of trying and learning something even from failure is progress or as Astro calls it “Learning moment” (Keep in mind, in the video Astro says it in a really strange way, and I am just interpreting the message that he was trying to explain. Hopefully I did it justice). The mindset of trying to better the world is awesome and I love this type of ideas, it’s something that I truly relate to, in one of my previous blogs I actually speak on this a bit and relay why I wish to work in Machine Learning, there is so much potential in this field to better the world, to create things that will help the whole population and give a positive impact on everybody not just myself, I just hope that I can be a fraction of who creative some ML engineers truly are! Which is an awesome segway into what most of this week’s focus was on. Yes machine learning is pretty freaking cool. That small ML tutorial was really detailed and explained concepts very well, I even remembered some stuff myself, it felt almost like someone was teaching me basic mathematics and remembering concepts correctly and confirming the knowledge I have. I highly recommend this tutorial for anyone wanting to learn a bit more about ML and maybe even better this could be a start of a beautiful new career path, just don’t get discouraged with the math involved! Now, speaking of Quantum Computing, is something i don’t really comprehend that much, I feel i understand the concept of superposition and what qubits are, but like Richard Feynman said: “If you think you understand quantum physics, you don't” which perfectly describes this new field but just thinking about what the future holds is super exciting, It’s amazing to think that one day in my lifetime i will be able to work with a quantum computer, and how our children and grandchildren will be doing incredible things with them if we guide them correctly, it’s all a matter of time!
