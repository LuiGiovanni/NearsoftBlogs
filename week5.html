Week 5
What i've learned

This week marks the beginning of our build something from scratch phase and the beginning of our project with our client, a REAL client. Now i can’t go into many details on the project or our client but, the project itself seems pretty interesting and how our mentors have told us, our project is one of the most interesting ideas in the bunch, i’m excited to get to work and communicate with our client, our team had some issues with how we were going to resolve the problem and our ideas were all over the place, in layman's terms, we were overthinking everything.

to be perfectly blunt and honest, we did not do a very good job this first week, I learned that as a team there must be more communication if we want to succeed in this project, sometime we fear of giving our opinion for various reasons maybe you're afraid of looking stupid, or don't want to speak up, or if you're like me you don't to offend or hurt others with you opinions, I found it really hard to express somethings to my team for this exact reason, but the truth is if you don't speak up in the beginning you'll pay for it down the road when things become a bit more challenging to fix and that's why it's important to not only trust in your team but also in yourself, I'm blessed to be with a team that I know I can say anything to, and express myself with them knowing I don't wish to hurt them or insult them, they trust me and of course I do as well.

Ofcourse, having great guides helps a lot. Not only our mentors, but staff were of incredible help, and most importantly patient, we are here to learn and this was no more true that this week, the mistakes were many and we learned from them the hard way, luckily we had staffs help to explain and guide us in the correct way of thinking and how to approach the problem, we must not think of this as simple problem solving and trying to find the solution just because we need to solve it, you have to believe that your clients problem is painful and something that is really hurting them, and if they hurt you get hurt as well, you have to understand that they are important, as people and for some reason at least for me, that inspired me to want to create something incredible something that would be so perfect and comfortable to my client.

In the end we seemed to arrive on a solution that worked for most part, there's not that much to talk about, we were let loose and the responsibilities fell solely on us, we set the meetings, we did the talking and the organization, yes we had a lot of help from our academy staff, but we finally get a glimpse of how working at Nearsoft Encora really is. A bit of a side note, more pressure placed on us thanks to the fact that this client is thinking about joining Encora Nearsoft as a client to stay, so basically we are the welcoming party, I plan on putting everything I got to represent Encora Nearsoft in a favorable manner.
